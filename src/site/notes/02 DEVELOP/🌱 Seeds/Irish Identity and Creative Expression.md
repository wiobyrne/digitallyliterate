# Irish Identity and Creative Expression

*Writing yourself into being through Irish mythology and landscape*

> Ancestral echoes weave through my soul, a saga of resilience, making me whole.

---

## The Concept

For pre-service teachers exploring identity, diversity, culture, and privilege, creative expression offers a powerful way to "write yourself into being."

This includes giving others a "walk into your world" to understand:
- Where you're coming from
- What your dreams are
- What setbacks you've had
- Where you want to go

---

## Four Prompts for Identity Exploration

### 1. Ancestral Echoes
Draw inspiration from Irish mythology or historical figures to explore how ancestral roots shape identity.

- Incorporate elements from Irish legends
- Explore struggles faced by past generations
- Write about the echoes of ancestors in your thoughts, dreams, and aspirations
- Include imagery from your own landscapes or mythology

### 2. Celtic Threads of Diversity
Incorporate Ireland's rich tapestry of culturesâ€”Celtic, Viking, Norman influences.

- Reflect on how this diversity contributes to your own identity
- Weave together threads of diversity
- Acknowledge different aspects that shape who you are

### 3. The Emerald Mosaic
Imagine Ireland as a mosaic of cultures, traditions, and stories.

- Use Irish poetry or landscapes as metaphors for identity facets
- Explore how personal experiences contribute to this vibrant mosaic
- Allow others to walk through the colors and textures of your world

### 4. Beyond the Horizon
Look to Irish poets who explored dreams and aspirations.

- Write about your own dreams, ambitions, and setbacks
- Use the metaphor of the horizon to symbolize your future
- Allow others to glimpse the landscape of your hopes

## Irish Myths and Stories for Inspiration

### The Children of Lir
A king's children transformed into swans by a jealous stepmother. Theme of transformation and enduring hardshipâ€”useful for exploring personal struggles and resilience.

### Cu Chulainn
Legendary hero known for incredible strength and bravery, facing numerous challenges. Parallels between the hero's trials and personal life struggles.

### The Great Famine (1845-1852)
Historical event causing immense suffering and migration. Explore impact on ancestors and how resilience has been passed down through generations.

### Easter Rising (1916)
Pivotal event marking the fight for independence. Stories of courage and determination echoing in aspirations for justice and equality.

### Brigid of Kildare
Prominent figure known for compassion and generosity. Explore values passed down through generations and how they shape identity.
