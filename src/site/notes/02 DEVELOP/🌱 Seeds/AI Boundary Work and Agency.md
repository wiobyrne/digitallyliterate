# AI Boundary Work and Agency

*How humans negotiate their relationship with AI systems*

> Agency is defined by friction. They worked harder than the machine.

---

## The Core Framework

When pre-service teachers interact with AI tools like NotebookLM, two distinct interaction profiles emerge:

### The Orchestrator (High Agency)
The interaction log looks like a messy, circular **loop**:
- Prompt the AI
- Read the AI's response
- Pause and **refuse** it
- Re-prompt with constraints: "Don't just list facts. Capture the tone."

The Orchestrator engages in a **Correction Loop**. They treat AI not as an oracle, but as a junior analyst whose work needs to be checked, challenged, and often rejected. Their agency is defined by **friction**.

### The Outsourcer (High Delegation)
The interaction log looks like a straight, vertical **line**:
- Prompt: "Help me study."
- AI offers a structure
- Student accepts immediately

No deletions, no re-prompts, zero friction. **Gravity takes over.** The student delegated entirely.
