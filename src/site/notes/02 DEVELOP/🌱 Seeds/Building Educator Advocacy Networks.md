# Building Educator Advocacy Networks

*Empowering educators to become informed advocates*

> Through research, dialogue, and action, we aim to amplify educator voices and ensure equitable educational opportunities for all.

---

## The Vision

Educator advocacy networks empower teachers and educational professionals to:
- Become informed advocates for educational policy
- Foster collaborative efforts at local, state, and national levels
- Influence policy through research, dialogue, and action

---

## Guiding Principles

| Principle | What It Means |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Equity and Inclusion** | Prioritize policies that promote inclusive practices and address systemic barriers |
| **Educator Empowerment** | Build knowledge and skills for policy engagement |
| **Collaborative Engagement** | Connect educators, administrators, community stakeholders, and policymakers |
| **Research-Driven Advocacy** | Ground advocacy in evidence and participatory research |

---

## Year One Focus Areas

### Building Foundation
- Unpack privilege, identity, and bias in personal and professional contexts
- Build understanding of power structures in education
- Develop shared language and frameworks

### Building Skills
- Learn to speak up legally and confidently
- Safeguard professional integrity while advocating
- Navigate policy systems and structures

### Building Community
- Connect educators across contexts
- Share strategies and support
- Amplify collective voice

---

## What the Network Supports

- **Learning and Advocacy:** Building knowledge about privilege, identity, bias, and power structures
- **Empowerment and Action:** Developing skills to advocate while protecting professional standing
- **Collaborative Networking:** Connecting educators, administrators, and stakeholders

---

## Invitation Model

When building networks:
- Make participation voluntary and opt-in
- Allow members to invite others who share the vision
- Create space for people to opt out gracefully
- Start with connection before asking for commitment

---

## Connection to Digital Self-determination

Educator advocacy networks align with digital self-determination by:
- Centering practitioner voice and agency
- Building capacity rather than dependency
- Using decentralized, privacy-respecting communication tools
- Modeling cooperative governance
