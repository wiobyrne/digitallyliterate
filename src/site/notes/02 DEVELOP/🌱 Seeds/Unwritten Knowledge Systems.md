# Unwritten Knowledge Systems

> Not all knowledge is written down. Some is heard in the pitch of a sizzle plate, felt in the palm of your hand, passed down in story, movement, and place.

---

## The Core Insight

In the rush to digitize everything, we forget that much of what humans know, create, and pass down is embedded in:

- **Gesture and movement**
- **Rhythm and sound**
- **Ritual and ceremony**
- **Oral storytelling**
- **Environmental cues**
- **Embodied practice**

These systems of meaning are powerful, complex, and deeply human â€” yet they're often rendered invisible in dominant technological narratives.

---

## The Mindset Behind Machines

Every technology carries a worldview. Most modern AI systems are built on:
- Data and logic
- Categorization and optimization
- What can be measured and stored

**What happens to knowledge that lives in breath, movement, place, or pause?**

The ethos behind much of today's AI risks flattening the vibrant, contextual, and communal dimensions of human communication.

## Questions for AI Development

- What might AI look like if designed with oral tradition in mind?
- What models would emerge from theories of language that center rhythm, story, or silence?
- How might design change if we took seriously the knowledge held in communities who have long communicated outside of print?

- What is being left out of our systems?
- Whose knowledge is being ignored because it's not written down?
