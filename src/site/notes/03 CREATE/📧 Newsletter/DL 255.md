### [America is not prepared for schools opening this fall. This will be bad.](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2020/jul/13/america-schools-coronavirus-covid-19-children)

There is nothing Americans can do to save public education right now. We had a window about three months ago. We saw this coming. Teachers all saw this coming. There was no federal help, no national leadership.

We got to visit bars and [amusement parks](https://twitter.com/TimSatre/status/1282014432105816066) this summer, though. So there's that.

This [COVID-19 Event Risk Assessment Planning Tool](https://covid19risk.biosci.gatech.edu/) provides interactive context to assess the risk that one or more individuals infected with COVID-19 are present in an event of various sizes.

### [Teachers: You Are Being Gaslit](https://medium.com/digital-diplomacy/teachers-you-are-being-gaslit-a2bb2e0fbdca)

We are being presented with the false choice between our own safety and quality education. We are being made to feel crazy for being scared to do our jobs, when in reality, it is logical to be scared.

You have the power to shut it down.

### [Stay Apart or Stay Home](https://insidehighered.com/news/2020/07/15/student-conduct-codes-and-pledges-promise-good-covid-19-habits)

Student conduct codes and pledges promise good COVID-19 habits.

Whether we call this a "community compact," "social contract" or "behavioral compact," it's time for educators to start thinking about the words you'll use to address this with your students. Educators are the prime (most important) connection with our students. Your life (and the lives of others) may depend on it.

What will you say?

### [How to Judge Whether Your School District Is Doing Enough](https://offspring.lifehacker.com/how-to-assess-whether-your-school-district-is-doing-eno-1844379276)

I'm sure no one could use this guidance at this point. ðŸ™ƒ

[This 62 page report](https://schools.forhealth.org/wp-content/uploads/sites/19/2020/06/Harvard-Healthy-Buildings-Program-Schools-For-Health-Reopening-Covid19-June2020.pdf) the Harvard School of Public Health's "[Healthy Buildings](https://forhealth.org/)" Program outlines recommendations for five aspects of in-person schooling:

- Healthy Classrooms
- Healthy Buildings
- Healthy Policies
- Healthy Schedules
- Healthy Activities

As we think about opening up the new academic year, we should be focusing on the students that need to attend F2F, as opposed to blanket policies. To that end, [this piece on CERPs](https://journals.uic.edu/ojs/index.php/fm/article/view/10845/9563) (COVID-19 exposure risk profile) is a mandatory read.

CERPs hinge on preexisting forms of social differentiation such as socioeconomic status, as individuals with more economic resources at their disposal can better insulate themselves from exposure risk.
