In TL;DR I'm sharing things that happened during the week in literacy, technology, & education that I think you should know. Please feel free to share with others you believe would benefit. If you like what you see here, I recommend subscribing (http://wiobyrne.us11.list-manage.com/subscribe?u=7b5ee323a16720dad25ff96ff&id=17e92a2ae1) to get it hand-delivered to your inbox.

To send me feedback, questions, concerns...please email me at wiobyrne@gmail.com or reach out on Twitter (https://twitter.com/wiobyrne) . To review past issues please click here (http://us11.campaign-archive1.com/home/?u=7b5ee323a16720dad25ff96ff&id=17e92a2ae1) . Thanks again for the support. :)

This week I worked on the following:

* Screencaptures & Screencasts in Education, Assessment, and Research (http://wiobyrne.com/screencaptures-screencasts-education-assessment-research/) - This week I taught my students about screencaptures and screencasts for an upcoming assignment. As a result, I thoroughly revised my previous posts on the subject. I think screencaptures/screencasts are mandatory tools that educators have in their toolkit.

* Allow it to be YouRTube (http://wiobyrne.com/yourtube/) - This post is another revision from a prior post. It is a guide for educators that are considering including online, digital video in their classroom...and how to get started. YouTube is one of those tools that educators sometimes feel they should be afraid of...and not using. This is especially true if we consider recording and publishing online. My thinking is that we should all be recording, remixing, and publishing digital content. YouTube is just one venue to help make this happen.

* Moving Toward Transparency in Higher Education (http://wiobyrne.com/moving-toward-transparency-in-higher-education/) - This post is from a document I wrote up with my colleague Amanda Bozack (https://www.linkedin.com/in/amanda-bozack-0104a5a0) . We were responding to the decisions, initiatives, and changes that were happening in higher ed all around us. We kept thinking that we should write something to get people to start thinking/talking about possibilities for transparency in relationships and management. Ultimately we too late realized that these decisions would cause the dissolution of our jobs and programs. Nevertheless, I have been thinking more about transparency and will have more of my thoughts in upcoming posts.

** Free speech or safe spaces? (http://www.slate.com/articles/news_and_politics/jurisprudence/2015/11/campus_protests_need_dialogue_not_safe_spaces_and_offense.html)

On our college campuses there recently has been a lot of protest and calls for recognition of a number of issues. In this post, ** Dalitha Lithwick (https://twitter.com/dahlialithwick)

calls for initiatives that promote dialogue and empathy among individuals.

I'm sharing this post to promote our thinking about online and hybrid spaces that could be leveraged as safe spaces for dialogue and unpacking these issues. The challenge is that, as I've detailed in the first couple of elements I shared in this newsletter...online spaces can sometimes be used for good or evil. Wondering if there is a way technology can be used to help.

** (http://www.psfk.com/2015/06/google-eavesdropping-tool-audio-snooping-code-privacy.html)

** Is bigger always better? The case for starting small with new learning ideas (http://www.npr.org/sections/ed/2015/11/28/452898967/is-bigger-always-better-the-case-for-starting-small-with-new-learning-ideas?utm_medium=RSS&utm_campaign=education)

This piece in ** nprEd (http://www.npr.org/sections/ed/)

shares an interview with Matt Candler and the ** 4.0 Schools initiative (http://4pt0.org/)

.

Although I am intrigued by the work and model from 4.0 Schools, I'm much more moved by the overall guidance of the post that suggests that it is perfectly appropriate and that you're being successful if you don't worry about scale and numbers all of the time.

While creating and sharing online, we sometimes are very aware of numbers of readers/viewers and wondering why we can't have thousands or millions more. Perhaps there is wisdom in focusing on small-scale approaches. As the post states, perhaps we should focus on communities, identify problems, come up with solutions, and test prototypes.

I will try to keep this advice in mind as I work on some new initiatives.
