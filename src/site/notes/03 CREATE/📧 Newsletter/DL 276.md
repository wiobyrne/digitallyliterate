### [GameStop Stock: The Complete Moron's Guide](https://arstechnica.com/gaming/2021/01/the-complete-morons-guide-to-gamestops-stock-roller-coaster/)

Last week, an epic short squeeze drove GameStop stock up to $40 a shareâ€”roughly 1,500 percent increase from its low point nine months ago.

This is just the beginning of the story, and an important narrative in Internet history. Vastly different from GamerGate and the Arab Spring, these stories all hold portends for the future.

A group of young traders has been propelled, and empowered, by technology to make some money, make a mockery of the markets, and perhaps [do it for the lulz](https://www.wired.com/2011/11/anonymous-101/).

They use tools like [Robinhood](https://robinhood.com/us/en/), a trading app with no transaction fees and a fun, user-friendly interface. They turn to online communities like Discord and Redditâ€”most notably [r/WallStreetBets](https://www.reddit.com/r/wallstreetbets/)â€”where foul-mouthed, meme-heavy discussions feel native to high school and college students.

Though most traders are too young to remember the 2008 financial crisis, they witnessed its impact on their families. They watched people struggle to make ends meet while losing jobs and homes. They are hyper-aware of income inequality and disparities in access to wealth in America.

For [deeper analysis](https://marketsweekly.ghost.io/what-happened-with-gamestop/), see [Alexis Goldstein's](https://twitter.com/alexisgoldstein) primer.

### [What the Arab Spring Can Teach Us About GameStop](https://www.wired.com/story/arab-spring-gamestop/)

It's difficult not to get swept up by the belief that activists using social-network tools could topple an oppressive regime. Yet rather than bringing democratic institutions to countries long denied them, the internet often works in reverseâ€”destabilizing democracy around the world and expanding inequality.

### [Formative Assessment Strategies](https://jaymctighe.com/)

[Jay McTighe](https://jaymctighe.com/) shares opportunities for formative assessment to guide learners and anticipate instruction:

- **Signal It**: Hand signal to indicate understanding
- **Choose It**: True/false, agree/disagree responses
- **Picture It**: Visual or graphical representations
- **Troubleshoot It**: Identify and correct the flaw
- **Summarize It**: Synthesize and condense information
- **Apply It**: Use information in a new context
- **Teach It**: Share with someone else
- **Analogize It**: Develop a metaphor to explain

Simple techniques that make thinking visible.
