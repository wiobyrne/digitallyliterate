** Why degrowth is the only responsible way forward (https://www.opendemocracy.net/en/oureconomy/why-degrowth-only-responsible-way-forward/)

------------------------------------------------------------

As we consider the impact of climate change, we need to understand that environmental issues are all interrelated. You should do your part, but understand the chief concern is not straws, plastic bags at the supermarket, meatless burgers, or cow farts. Much of the root cause of this crisis is rampant capitalism (https://www.wired.com/story/capitalocene/) . Since 1988, 100 corporations have been responsible for 70 percent of greenhouse gas emissions (https://www.theguardian.com/sustainable-business/2017/jul/10/100-fossil-fuel-companies-investors-responsible-71-global-emissions-cdp-study-climate-change) .

Technological shifts always come at an environmental cost. Every sector of our economy is still based on some form of extraction, pollution, and waste. And all of them depend on carbon. Renewable energy, in particular, requires a great amount of rare minerals (https://earth.stanford.edu/news/critical-minerals-scarcity-could-threaten-renewable-energy-future#gs.1syz3k) and land-use. The same goes for nuclear energy, which demands considerable resources (https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0360544212008754) in order to mine uranium, construct power plants, and deal with its

waste. Even digital technology has environmental impacts (https://www.custommade.com/blog/carbon-footprint-of-internet/) .

To sustain the natural basis of our life, we must slow down. We have to reduce the amount of extraction, pollution, and waste throughout our economy. This implies less production, less consumption, less work (https://www.vice.com/en_us/article/bj9yjq/the-radical-plan-to-save-the-planet-by-working-less) , and degrowth.

Thanks to Doug Belshaw (https://dougbelshaw.com/) for sharing many of these links in his Thought Shrapnel networks. (https://thoughtshrapnel.com/2019/09/21/saturday-strikings/)
