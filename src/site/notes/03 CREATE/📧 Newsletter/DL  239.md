Last week I talked about the [Online Learning Collective Facebook Group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/onlinelearningcollective/) that I helped start up. ### [Don't Lose High Touch When Moving to High Tech](https://www.kqed.org/mindshift/55608/14-tips-for-helping-students-with-limited-internet-have-distance-learning)

Here's 5 ways to keep it human:

- Simplify and be flexible
- Don't assume people have reliable tech, or understand how to use it
- Look for ways to build community
- Don't be afraid to crowdsource ideas
- Keep the big picture in mind

I recommend this resource on [14 tips for connecting with others that have limited Internet resources](https://www.kqed.org/mindshift/55608/14-tips-for-helping-students-with-limited-internet-have-distance-learning).

It's important that we think first about [student agency](https://www.insidehighered.com/blogs/university-venus/student-agency-uncertain-times) in these times. This [series of interviews](https://www.insidehighered.com/digital-learning/article/2020/03/25/how-shift-remote-learning-might-affect-students-instructors-and#.XntGmu6H4cc.twitter) with experts in the field provides valuable reflection.

### [That Discomfort You're Feeling Is Grief](https://hbr.org/2020/03/that-discomfort-youre-feeling-is-grief)

It's important to acknowledge the grief you may be feeling, how to manage it, and how we can find meaning in it. We all have different levels of grief and express it in different ways.

Ways to acknowledge and deal with this anxiety and grief:

- Find balance in the things you're thinking
- Calm yourself by coming into the present
- Let go of what you can't control
- Stock up on compassion

### [Make "Work From Home" Work for You](https://www.blog.google/inside-google/googlers/make-work-from-home-work-for-you/)

This provides great principles for making "work from home" work. You can find similar info [here](https://www.theguardian.com/education/2020/mar/26/how-to-study-at-home-during-coronavirus-by-online-students-and-tutors) in a different context.

**#1 tip for mental health: Work at home doesn't mean working all of the time.**

- Designate your "spot" for working
- Use video chat like a pro
- Practice "one tab working"
- Act the part
- Play with your schedule and energy
- Don't work all of the time
- Create "to-do list" the day before
- Finish one thing per day
- Cut yourself some slack

### [How to Clean and Disinfect All Your Gadgets](https://www.howtogeek.com/663058/how-to-clean-and-disinfect-all-your-gadgets/)

Make time in your schedule to clean and disinfect all of your gadgets this weekend. Seriously. We need you to stay healthy at home.

Whether you want to protect against COVID-19 or just give all your gadgets a deep-clean while you're stuck at home, now's the ideal time.
