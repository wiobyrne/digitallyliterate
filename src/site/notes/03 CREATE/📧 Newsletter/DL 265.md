### [Justice Department Sues Google for Antitrust](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/10/20/technology/google-antitrust.html)

On Tuesday, the Justice Department [sued Google](https://www.wsj.com/articles/justice-department-to-file-long-awaited-antitrust-suit-against-google-11603195203) for anticompetitive practices, in the most significant antitrust action by the U.S. government against a technology company in decades. The government's case focused on Google's search and how it appeared to create a monopoly through exclusive business contracts.

Google [responded](https://blog.google/outreach-initiatives/public-policy/response-doj/) that this was a deeply flawed lawsuit that would only hurt consumers.

Karl Bode in TechDirt [suggests](https://www.techdirt.com/articles/20201020/09563045541/bill-barrs-google-antitrust-inquiry-is-weaponized-farce.shtml) that this is a weaponized farce from Bill Barr before the election.

Ben Thompson offers a more measured review while explaining [Aggregation Theory](https://stratechery.com/concept/aggregation-theory/), which is endemic to digital markets. Read more at his original [Aggregation Theory Article](https://stratechery.com/2015/aggregation-theory/).

Pay attention and educate yourself. You need to read up on aggregation theoryâ€”you'll need it on the quiz.

### [Proctorio Trying to Silence Critics With Lawsuits](https://www.vice.com/en/article/7k9zjy/an-exam-surveillance-company-is-trying-to-silence-critics-with-lawsuits)

Between August 23rd and 24th, [Ian Linkletter](https://twitter.com/Linkletter), a learning technology specialist at the University of British Columbia, began researching and tweeting about Proctorio, an online test-proctoring tool. Linkletter has become the [target of a lawsuit](https://www.theverge.com/2020/10/22/21526792/proctorio-online-test-proctoring-lawsuit-universities-students-coronavirus), and says he has drained his savings while fighting back against the company's attempts to silence him.

You might consider donating to the [GoFundMe page](https://www.gofundme.com/f/stand-against-proctorio/) set up by Linkletter to fight this lawsuit.

Read more [from Stephen Downes](https://www.downes.ca/post/71320).

### [2020 Student Technology Report](https://library.educause.edu/resources/2020/10/2020-student-technology-report-supporting-the-whole-student)

This report presents results from EDUCAUSE's 2020 research on students' experience with information technology, which included 16,162 undergraduate students from 71 US institutions.

Findings identify next steps institutions can take in student success, technology use, data privacy, online harassment, and accessibility.

In the remaining weeks before the 2020 US Elections, Twitter is limiting your ability to retweet content from others. More specifically, Twitter is urging you to give some context in your re-shareâ€”as opposed to blindly boosting the message of others.

### [Butterfly Attack: The Origins of Fake Social Media Accounts](https://mediamanipulation.org/case-studies/butterfly-attack-origins-fake-antifa-social-media-accounts)

As I follow online information trends, it's interesting to see the terms used to describe many of these practices. Here's some new info for your lexicon.

Throughout 2017, pranksters and extremists utilized parody accounts to discredit the antifascist movement in the US, taking advantage of available Twitter handles and public confusion to spread narratives about antifa violence. These [butterfly attacks](http://mediamanipulation.org/definitions/butterfly-attack) used [keyword squatting](https://mediamanipulation.org/definitions/keyword-squatting) to capture attention during breaking news events.
