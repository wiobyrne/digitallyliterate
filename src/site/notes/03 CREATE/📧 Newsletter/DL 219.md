** Under digital surveillance: how American schools spy on millions of kids (https://www.theguardian.com/world/2019/oct/22/school-student-surveillance-bark-gaggle)

------------------------------------------------------------

Fueled by fears of school shootings, the market has grown rapidly for technologies that monitor students through official school emails and chats.

There is still no independent evaluation of whether this kind of surveillance technology actually works to reduce violence and self-harm. Privacy experts say pervasive monitoring may hurt children, and may be particularly dangerous for students with disabilities and students of color.

Despite the lack of research evidence, tech companies are marketing school monitoring technologies with bold claims of hundreds of lives saved, mostly through prevention of youth suicide attempts.

** Kids these days: Why the youth of today seem lacking (https://advances.sciencemag.org/content/5/10/eaav5916.full)

------------------------------------------------------------

An assessment of people‚Äôs tendency to believe ‚Äúkids these days‚Äù are deficient relative to those of previous generations.

Findings suggest that denigrating today‚Äôs youth is a fundamental illusion grounded in several cognitive mechanisms, including a specific bias to see others as lacking in those domains on which one excels and a memory bias projecting one‚Äôs current traits to past generations.

Two mechanisms contribute to humanity‚Äôs perennial tendency to denigrate kids: (1) a person-specific tendency to notice the limitations of others where one excels, (ii) a memory bias projecting one‚Äôs current qualities onto the youth of the past.

In short, adults (especially in authoritative contexts) are more willing to ignore/denigrate the qualities of others, while comparing this to our biased & romanticized version of our own upbringing. No decline exists.

* Desert Island (https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.digitalwellbeingexperiments.desertisland) helps you focus on only the essential apps you need for a certain task or on a particular day.
