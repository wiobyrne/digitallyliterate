### [From Online Memes to Real-World Violence](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2020/10/08/michigan-plot-kidnapping-boogaloo-socialmedia/)

This week, 13 people were charged with a [plot to kidnap Michigan Governor Gretchen Whitmer](https://www.wxyz.com/news/fbi-michigan-ag-to-announce-details-of-major-operation-behind-raid-at-hartland-home). The Wolverine Watchmen sought to [instigate civil war](https://www.detroitnews.com/story/news/local/michigan/2020/10/08/feds-thwart-militia-plot-kidnap-michigan-gov-gretchen-whitmer/5922301002/) by kidnapping the Governor and having her stand trial for treason.

We should have known this was coming as we see this wave of extremist groups radicalized online. A line of rage that flows from online memes to real-world violence.

[Charlie Warzel](https://twitter.com/cwarzel) pulls these threads together and lays the blame at the feet of Zuckerberg and Facebook: With every bit of friction Facebook introduces, our information ecosystem becomes a bit less unstable. Flip that logic around and the conclusion is unsettling. Facebook, when it's working as designed, is a natural accelerating force in the erosion of our shared reality.

Irony-dependent meme culture has flourished over the last 10 years, with racism and sexism often explained away as simple viral humor. But the path jokes took into the mainstreamâ€”originating on 4Chan before being laundered for the public sphere by journalistsâ€”is the same route now used to spread QAnon, health misinformation, and targeted abuse.

### [QAnon Shows the Age of Alternative Facts Will Not End with Trump](https://www.cjr.org/opinion/qanon-trump-alternative-facts.php)

[Alice Marwick](https://twitter.com/alicetiara) and [Will Partin](https://twitter.com/william_partin) on QAnon and the growing trend of people believing in things that are wrong.

[QAnon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/QAnon) believers call themselves "bakers" and turn "crumbs" of information from Q into "proofs" which are then "bread" or worthy research. Marwick and Partin connect this group and their practices to Henry Jenkins's notion of [participatory culture](https://www.google.com/books/edition/Fans_Bloggers_and_Gamers/jj2eKl3NcBEC) and ask how is this any different than Star Trek or Harry Potter fans.

### [Preparing for Misinformation Conversations](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2020/06/05/stop-spreading-misinformation/)

Preparing for misinformation might mean decluttering your feed, or making some suggestions to your friends and family.

Practice saying this: "Like you, I am concerned about the XXXX. Like you, I am concerned about the direction of this XXXX. However, there are other sources out there that may dispute some of the facts and dispute some of the stuff that you're talking about."
