### [Facebook and the Group That Planned to Kidnap Gretchen Whitmer](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/10/08/opinion/facebook-gretchen-whitmer.html)

This week, [13 people were charged with a plot to kidnap Michigan Governor Gretchen Whitmer](https://www.wxyz.com/news/fbi-michigan-ag-to-announce-details-of-major-operation-behind-raid-at-hartland-home). The *Wolverine Watchmen* [sought to instigate civil war](https://www.detroitnews.com/story/news/local/michigan/2020/10/08/feds-thwart-militia-plot-kidnap-michigan-gov-gretchen-whitmer/5922301002/) by kidnapping the Governor and having her stand trial for treason.

We should have known this was coming as we see this wave of extremist groups radicalized online. [A line of rage that flows from online memes to real-world violence](https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2020/10/08/michigan-plot-kidnapping-boogaloo-socialmedia/).

[Charlie Warzel](https://twitter.com/cwarzel) pulls these threads together and lays the blame of the feet of Zuckerberg and Facebook.

> With every bit of friction Facebook introduces to its platform, our information ecosystem becomes a bit less unstable. Flip that logic around and the conclusion is unsettling. Facebook, when it's working as designed, is a natural accelerating force in the erosion of our shared reality and, with it, our democratic norms.

### [How the truth was murdered](https://www.technologyreview.com/2020/10/07/1009336/how-the-truth-was-murdered-disinformation-abuse-harassment-online-2/)

Pandemic, protest, and a precarious election have created an overwhelming flood of disinformation. [Abby Ohlheiser](https://twitter.com/abbyohlheiser) on why it didn't have to be this way.

> Irony-dependent meme culture has flourished over the last 10 years, with the racism and sexism often explained away by white reporters as simple viral humor. But the path jokes took into the mainstream, originating on message boards like 4Chan before being laundered for the public sphere by journalists, is the same route now used to spread QAnon, health misinformation, and targeted abuse. The way reporters covered memes helped teach white supremacists exactly how much they could get away with.

### [QAnon shows that the age of alternative facts will not end with Trump](https://www.cjr.org/opinion/qanon-trump-alternative-facts.php)

[Alice Marwick](https://twitter.com/alicetiara) and [Will Partin](https://twitter.com/william_partin) on QAnon, and the growing trend of people believing in things that are wrong.

[QAnon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/QAnon) is a right-wing conspiracy premised on the idea that Donald Trump is working with military intelligence to bring down a global ring of child-eating pedophiles. Q researchers call themselves "bakers" and turn "crumbs" of information from Q (an anonymous account who claims to have insider knowledge of the Trump administration). The "bakers" research, aggregate, and combine these "crumbs" into "proofs" which are then "bread" or a form of worthy research.

In this, QAnon believers think they are paving the way for the "Great Awakening," an earth-shattering event in which all of Trump's enemies will be arrested for being Satan-worshipping pedophiles.

Marwick and Partin connect this group and their practices to Henry Jenkins's notion of [*participatory culture*](https://www.google.com/books/edition/Fans_Bloggers_and_Gamers/jj2eKl3NcBEC?hl=en&gbpv=0) and ask how is this any different than Star Trek or Harry Potter fans.
