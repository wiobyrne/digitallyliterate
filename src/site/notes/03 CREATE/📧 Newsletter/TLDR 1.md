** [Americans' Internet Access: 2000-2015](http://www.pewinternet.org/2015/06/26/americans-internet-access-2000-2015/)

As internet use nears saturation for some groups, a look at patterns of adoption.

Key takeaways - the Internet is blowing up. :) The digital divides based on race, ethnicity, and education appear to be shrinking. Read the [full report here](http://www.pewinternet.org/files/2015/06/2015-06-26_internet-usage-across-demographics-discover_FINAL.pdf).

** [Is Google listening to you?](http://www.psfk.com/2015/06/google-eavesdropping-tool-audio-snooping-code-privacy.html)

Another round of technopanic is spreading through the Internet as the open source version of Chrome was found to have some of the same [audio capture code](http://www.psfk.com/2015/06/google-eavesdropping-tool-audio-snooping-code-privacy.html) that is contained in Chrome. In a [further examination of the code](http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/google-eavesdropping-chromium-users/), it appears that Google made a mistake and is addressing it. The key takeaway is that our devices are listening to us. We need to know and understand what they are doing. Teach your kids to code. :)

** Sesame Street was the first MOOC?!?!

Interesting back and forth here. University of Maryland's Melissa Kearney and Wellesley College's Phillip Levine [show possible replacement](https://www.edsurge.com/n/2015-06-09-the-original-mooc-can-sesame-street-replace-preschool) of pre-school through [viewing of Sesame Street](http://www.washingtonpost.com/business/economy/sesame-street-and-its-surprisingly-powerful-effects-on-how-children-learn/2015/06/07/59c73fe4-095c-11e5-9e39-0db921c47b93_story.html). The Brookings Institute [calls Sesame Street the first MOOC](http://www.brookings.edu/blogs/brookings-now/posts/2015/06/sesame-street-was-the-original-mooc). [Audrey Watters](http://hackeducation.com/2015/06/21/sesame-street-is-not-a-mooc-ffs/) and [Stephen Downes](http://www.downes.ca/cgi-bin/page.cgi?post=64063) quickly debunk that thinking.
