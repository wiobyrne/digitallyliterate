### [NFT Explainer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nft-explainer)

This is probably one of the best (although satirical) explainers for NFTs.

NFT stands for Non-Fungible Token. NFTs are digital assetsâ€”publicly verifiable intellectual property authenticated on a blockchain.

Fungible means something is able to be exchanged or substituted and will hold the same value. It's interchangeable like the dollar, gold, casino chips, bitcoin, or frequent flyer points.

Non-fungible means uniqueâ€”this specific thing, not interchangeable with another.

### [Amazon: Parasitic Opportunism](https://www.theatlantic.com/amazon-rise)

Rather than a story of innovation that makes Amazon's rise inevitable, this may be a story of "parasitic opportunism, filling the spaces left by the decline of American manufacturing and taking advantage of industrial consolidation."

As many parts of society hurt, Amazon is in the headlines amid a [high-stakes union election](https://www.vice.com/en/article/wx8mb5/the-unionizing-bessemer-alabama-workers-who-became-amazons-biggest-threat) at an Alabama warehouse.

This brought attention to the [great lengths Amazon delivery drivers go through to pee](https://www.vice.com/en/article/akd54e/she-wees-and-plastic-bags-amazons-pee-scandal-is-much-worse-for-women), resulting in a [Twitter war](https://www.vox.com/recode/2021/3/28/22354604/amazon-twitter-bernie-sanders-jeff-bezos-union-alabama-elizabeth-warren) between Amazon CEO and critics.

To address challenges, Amazon amassed an [army of "ambassadors" defending the corporation online](https://theintercept.com/2021/03/30/amazon-twitter-ambassadors-jeff-bezos-bernie-sanders/).

### [The Little-Known Data Broker Industry Is Spending Big Bucks Lobbying Congress](https://themarkup.org/privacy/2021/04/01/the-little-known-data-broker-industry-is-spending-big-bucks-lobbying-congress)

Data brokers collect information from public records, online activity, and purchase history and re-sell it to other companies for marketing. The more companies know about consumers, the more targeted their advertisements.

Data brokers have made fortunes from collecting and sharing millions of people's personal information while flying under the radar. They might not be [familiar to most individuals](https://www.fastcompany.com/90310803/here-are-the-data-brokers-quietly-buying-and-selling-your-personal-information), but they're making themselves known on Capitol Hill.

Data broker lobbying spending in 2020 rivaled that of some Big Tech firms.
