### [Asimov's Three Laws Need Four More](https://onezero.medium.com/its-time-to-add-4-new-laws-of-robotics-8791139cdb11)

An interview with [Frank Pasquale](https://twitter.com/FrankPasquale), author of [New Laws of Robotics](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674975224).

[Asimov's three laws of robotics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Laws_of_Robotics) are the most famous science fictional lines of tech policy ever written. Pasquale says we must push further with four new ones:

1. Digital technologies ought to "complement professionals, not replace them."
2. A.I. and robotic systems "should not counterfeit humanity."
3. A.I. should be prevented from intensifying "zero-sum arms races."
4. Robotic and A.I. systems need to "indicate the identity of their creator(s), controller(s), and owner(s)."

### [Why Are Public Thinkers Flocking to Substack?](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2020/nov/17/substack-media-platform-publishing)

This newsletter started in Mailchimp. I've been slowly pulling away and archiving issues on a website. I spent time looking at Substack, but ultimately decided against it. It reminds me of the hype around [Medium](https://medium.com/) when it first started.
