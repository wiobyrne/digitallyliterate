### [Musicians hear songs when they read music, non-musicians seek visual patterns](http://arstechnica.com/science/2015/09/musicians-hear-songs-when-they-read-music-non-musicians-seek-visual-patterns/)

Over the past couple of TL;DR issues I've shared some research and posts discussing what our brains "see" while reading. In this post from [ArsTechnica](http://arstechnica.com/), [research presented in PNAS](http://www.pnas.org/content/early/2015/09/09/1510662112) discuss "training-related plasticity" in our brains. ### [This is what the Internet looks like](http://www.wired.com/2015/09/internet-looks-like-irl/)

Most of us take the Internet for granted, and could care less about how it gets here. [Heinrich Holtgreve](http://holtgreve.org/) doesn't appear to be one of those people.

For the past three years, the photographer has been capturing and sharing the spaces and places that are used to connect us. His series titled [The Internet as a Place](http://holtgreve.org/gallery/is-it-a-box-the-internet-as-a-place/) takes you on a thought-provoking journey of the cables, routers, and black boxes that bring us our cat videos and GIFs.

For something simpler...check out this collection of patent drawings detailing [the shape of the Internet](http://noahveltman.com/internet-shape/).

### [Situating Makerspaces in Schools](http://www.hybridpedagogy.com/journal/situating-makerspaces-in-schools/)

Quite a bit has been shared over the past week in regards to makers and hackers in society and or schools. This piece from [Colin Angevine](https://twitter.com/colinangevine) and [Josh Weisgrau](https://twitter.com/jweisgrau) in [Hybrid Pedagogy](http://www.hybridpedagogy.com/) discuss the need for focusing on pedagogy in these spaces.

**The summary:**

> Laser cutters and 3D printers are expensive tools for schools just as desktop PCs once were. And yet, in the interests of supporting STEM education, schools continue to find funding for high-tech tools. Computers in schools have largely became very expensive spell checkers for writing essays. We can't allow new tools for digital fabrication to become the facilitators of ever flashier dioramas. Defining making in education in terms of tools, spaces, or disciplines is insufficient. Learning through making is a philosophical approach that can affect classes across the curriculum and schools across the globe. It's time to change the paradigm.

### [Six ways to tell if that viral story is a hoax](http://gizmodo.com/six-easy-ways-to-tell-if-that-viral-story-is-a-hoax-1732026737)

I've done a lot of research on critical evaluation of online information. Heck...it was the focus of my dissertation. With that being said...these search mechanisms to evaluate the credibility and relevance of online content are beyond me. I definitely think you should pick up these links and skills and incorporate into your repertoire.

I don't think this is a good way to see if your students are copy/pasting their papers. I do think it's a great way to have your students build their critical media literacy skills in class.

### [MoocNote - Add timestamped questions and comments to videos](http://www.freetech4teachers.com/2015/09/moocnote-add-timestamped-questions-and.html)

I'm a big fan of Vialogues to add dialogue and build a discussion room around a video. I've been [testing it with students](http://wiobyrne.com/using-vialogues-to-scaffold-student-use-of-video-and-dialogue-in-the-classroom/) and they seem to like it as well. MoocNote seems like an interesting new product to test out for the same purposes.

MoocNote will allow you to [add timestamped comments, questions, and links to videos](http://www.educatorstechnology.com/2015/09/Moocnote-add-notes-to-videos.html). Much of this is already accomplished by Vialogues. I'll continue to test it to see if the user interface is easier to use.
