### [Apple Is Spying on Your Mac](https://sneak.berlin/20201112/your-computer-isnt-yours/)

Apple announced an update to Mac OS this week. Mac OS X is deadâ€”welcome to [macOS 11.0 Big Sur](https://arstechnica.com/gadgets/2020/11/macos-11-0-big-sur-the-ars-technica-review/).

Jeffrey Paul indicates that in the current version, the OS sends to Apple a hash (unique identifier) of each and every program you run, when you run it.

This means Apple knows when you're at home. When you're at work. What apps you open there, and how often. They know when you open Premiere over at a friend's house on their Wi-Fi, and they know when you open Tor Browser in a hotel on a trip to another city.

The day that [Stallman](https://www.gnu.org/philosophy/right-to-read.en.html) and [Doctorow](https://craphound.com/pc/download/) have been warning us about has finally arrived.

### [A Game Designer's Analysis Of QAnon](https://medium.com/curiouserinstitute/a-game-designers-analysis-of-qanon-580972548be5)

We've talked about QAnon many times. In a previous issue I asked how QAnon is similar/different from other affinity groups like [Harry Potter fans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Potter_fandom) and [Trekkies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trekkie).

[Reed Berkowitz](https://twitter.com/soi), director of the [Curiouser Institute](http://www.curiouserinstitute.com/content/about-director), has experience developing games and indicates the commonalities.

QAnon has often been compared to ARGs and LARPs and rightly so. It uses many of the same gaming mechanisms and rewards. However this beast is very very different from a game.

Berkowitz labels this a **guided apophenia**. [Apophenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apophenia) is "the tendency to perceive a connection or meaningful pattern between unrelated or random things."
