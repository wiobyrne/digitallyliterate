## 📚 Readings & Insights

### 1. **Threads and Decentralized Networks**
Meta launched Threads as a Twitter competitor, with promises of federation and ActivityPub support. This could allow decentralized networks like Mastodon to interact with Threads users in the future. [Learn more](https://mastodon.example).

**Why this matters:** Threads’ success depends on Meta’s approach to privacy, security, and data usage.

---

### 2. **Inquiry-Based Objectives in Education**
Philippa Hardman outlines a framework for assessment in an AI-driven classroom:
- **Write inquiry-based objectives** to foster critical thinking and collaboration.
- **Design real-world projects** for practical application.
- **Create performance-based assessments** that measure both knowledge and skills.

**Why this matters:** Shifting to inquiry-based objectives prepares learners for critical engagement in a post-AI world. [Read more](https://www.example.com).

---

### 3. **Generative Textbooks and Active Reading**
David Wiley explores how generative AI could transform textbooks, making them dynamic and interactive. This approach could help learners navigate dense materials more effectively. [Explore Wiley’s insights](https://www.example.com).

**Why this matters:** Generative textbooks can support active reading and personalized learning experiences.

---

### 4. **Teaching Kids About Sexism**
Children develop gender stereotypes as early as age two. Teaching them about sexism helps recognize bias and encourages equality. Parents can:
- Define sexism in child-friendly terms.
- Provide real-world examples.
- Teach children to speak up against inequality.

**Why this matters:** Early interventions can foster a more equitable mindset in future generations.

---

### 5. **Compounding Progress**
Morgan Housel highlights how small ideas grow into big changes through unseen incremental growth. Progress involves merging and remixing ideas over time.

**Why this matters:** Staying optimistic about technological and societal progress helps envision a future that builds on collective growth.

3. **Adopt an Abundance Mindset**: See the world as full of opportunities.

5. **Spend Mental Energy Wisely**: Focus on what’s within your control.
