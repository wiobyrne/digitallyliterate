# DL 371

## A Soft Apocalypse of Truth

Welcome to _Digitally Literate_, issue #371. This week, I reflected on generative AI, privacy concerns, and the importance of fostering mindfulness and critical thinking.

---

## 🔖 Key Takeaways
- **Generative AI Hype**: Challenge the exaggerated claims and assess the societal risks of generative AI tools.
- **Privacy and Data**: Carefully weigh the trade-offs when sharing personal data.
- **Mindfulness Practices**: Interoceptive journaling offers a pathway to greater emotional regulation and self-awareness.

## 📚 Readings & Insights

### 1. **Generative AI: Hype vs. Reality**
Alex Hanna and Emily M. Bender critique the generative AI hype cycle in _Tech Policy Press_. They argue that these systems lack true comprehension and pose risks to workers, quality, and accountability. [Read more](https://techpolicy.press).

**Why this matters:** Without proper regulation, society risks a two-tier system where the wealthy access human services, and others settle for artificial substitutes.

---

### 2. **Eyeball Scanning for Crypto?**
Worldcoin’s global initiative to collect eyeball scans for cryptocurrency raises significant privacy concerns. Critics highlight deceptive practices and the invasive nature of the technology. [Explore the controversy](https://worldcoin.com).

**Why this matters:** Be cautious about trading personal biometric data for digital currency or services.

---

### 3. **The Six Types of Power and AI**
Laura Hilliger examines how AI reshapes the six types of power identified by French and Raven: legitimate, reward, coercive, referent, expert, and informational power. [Learn more](https://digitallyliterate.net).

**Why this matters:** Cognitive liberty is critical to understanding and navigating AI’s impact on power structures.

---

### 4. **Interoceptive Journaling for Mindfulness**
This mindfulness practice involves documenting physical sensations to enhance self-awareness and emotional regulation. A few minutes a day can lead to improved well-being. [Try it here](https://digitallyliterate.net).

**Why this matters:** Strengthen your connection to your body’s signals for better emotional health.

---

### 5. **What Tasks Should We Automate?**
Dan Nott and Scott Cambo’s comic in _The Nib_ questions which tasks should be automated and what value manual labor provides in a world dominated by AI. Elan Ullendorff expands on this in a related thread. [Explore the comic](https://thenib.com).

**Why this matters:** As automation advances, we must prioritize tasks that preserve human creativity, ambiguity, and critical thinking.

---

## 🛠️ DO: Examine Your Thoughts

Andrew James Taggart builds on Guo Gu’s framework of three types of thoughts:  
1. **Scattered Thoughts**: Let go of random distractions to foster focus.  
2. **Discursive Thoughts**: Examine the root causes of recurring stories, then release them.  
3. **Subtle Thoughts**: Observe and understand nuanced emotional states for greater clarity.  

Practice mindful observation to gain insight into your thought patterns.
