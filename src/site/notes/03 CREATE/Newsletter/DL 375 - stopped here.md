# DL 375 - stopped here

## Uncomfortable and Inconvenient Truths

Welcome to _Digitally Literate_, issue #375. This week, I reflected on the growing concerns around censorship, privacy, and the unintended consequences of algorithmic amplification.

---

## 🔖 Key Takeaways
- **Genetic Privacy**: Addressing the risks and responsibilities in safeguarding sensitive genetic data.
- **AI Repression**: Examining the rise of algorithm-driven control and the societal consequences of AI disinformation.
- **Censorship and Empathy**: Investigating the implications of book bans and fostering understanding through literature.

---

## 📚 Readings & Insights

### 1. **JuMBOs: A New Type of Astronomical Object?**
Scientists have identified Jupiter Mass Binary Objects (JuMBOs), smaller gaseous entities orbiting each other over vast distances. These objects offer new insights into planetary formation and celestial mechanics. [Explore the discovery](https://digitallyliterate.net).

**Why this matters:** Understanding these objects expands our knowledge of the cosmos and may provide clues about planetary evolution.

---

### 2. **PRIME: Online Algorithms and Social Learning**
Social media platforms prioritize content that sustains PRIME engagement—Prestigious, In-group, Moral, and Emotional information. This amplification fosters misperceptions, polarization, and the spread of misinformation. [Learn more](https://digitallyliterate.net).

**Why this matters:** Algorithms must be redesigned to support accurate and constructive social learning rather than fueling conflict and division.

---

### 3. **Freedom is Less Free**
The latest _Freedom on the Net_ report highlights how advancements in AI are enhancing digital repression globally. AI-generated disinformation is becoming harder to detect and mitigate, threatening freedom of expression. [Read the report](https://freedomhouse.org).

**Why this matters:** As AI capabilities grow, so does the need for robust mechanisms to combat disinformation and protect digital freedoms.

---

### 4. **23andMe Data Breach**
Hackers accessed millions of 23andMe accounts by exploiting weak user credentials and the platform’s features. The breach highlights vulnerabilities in genetic data privacy and raises questions about corporate transparency. [Investigate further](https://digitallyliterate.net).

**Why this matters:** This incident underscores the need for stronger safeguards for sensitive genetic information and increased accountability from companies.

---

### 5. **BookLooks and the Rise of Book Bans**
BookLooks.org, a site linked to Moms for Liberty, has become a resource for activists seeking to ban books addressing LGBTQ+ issues and racism. While claiming to provide objective reviews, the site has fueled widespread censorship efforts. [Learn more](https://digitallyliterate.net).

**Why this matters:** Book bans harm access to diverse perspectives and suppress critical conversations essential for societal progress.

---

### 6. **Fahrenheit 451 and Censorship**
danah boyd reflects on _Fahrenheit 451_ and its depiction of censorship and societal control. Bradbury’s narrative compels readers to consider the structural causes of repression beyond its visible effects. [Explore the analysis](https://digitallyliterate.net).

**Why this matters:** Understanding the roots of censorship helps us address systemic issues that enable the suppression of knowledge and expression.

If you’re stuck dwelling on past mistakes or embarrassing moments, try these strategies:

2. **Foster Self-compassion**: Shift your perspective to view yourself with kindness and understanding.
3. **Write Strategically**: Journal your thoughts to process emotional responses and reframe beliefs.
