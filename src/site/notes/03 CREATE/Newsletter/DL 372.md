# DL 372

## More Human Than Human

Welcome to _Digitally Literate_, issue #372. This week, I explored the intersections of AI, education, workplace dynamics, and the science of charisma and memory.

---

## 🔖 Key Takeaways
- **AI in Education**: AI should enhance, not replace, the critical role of educators in schools.
- **Workplace Surveillance**: Advances in "little tech" challenge worker privacy and autonomy.
- **Charisma and AI**: What if AI could replicate and enhance human charisma?
- **Memory Optimization**: A new theory suggests our brains organize memories based on future utility.

## 📚 Readings & Insights

### 1. **Workplace Surveillance and "Little Tech"**
The pandemic-driven shift to remote work has intensified workplace surveillance. From monitoring software to intrusive policies, these practices threaten worker privacy and rights. Read more about _little tech_ and workforce surveillance [here](https://home.coworker.org/worktech/).

**Why this matters:** Workplace technology should empower, not oppress, employees. Current trends risk eroding autonomy and fairness.

---

### 2. **Charisma and AI**
Joe Zadeh examines charisma's measurable components and its potential to be analyzed and replicated by AI. [Learn more](https://digitallyliterate.net).

**Why this matters:** If AI can replicate charisma, what are the implications for leadership, influence, and societal dynamics?

---

### 3. **Regulating Artificial Intelligence**
A primer on AI regulation offers accessible insights into balancing innovation and oversight. The resource emphasizes the need for a shared understanding of technology to navigate future challenges. [Explore the primer](https://digitallyliterate.net).

**Why this matters:** Clear guidelines are essential to align AI development with human values and societal needs.

---

### 4. **Organizing Memories for the Future**
New research in _Nature Neuroscience_ suggests that our brains categorize memories by their potential usefulness in novel situations. This theory builds on the dual-learning system of the hippocampus and neocortex. [Read the study](https://digitallyliterate.net).

**Why this matters:** Understanding memory optimization could enhance learning strategies and decision-making.

## 🛠️ DO: Focus on Impacts, Not Just Technical Details

When evaluating or regulating AI, emphasize societal impacts over technical intricacies. Start with these steps:
1. Identify the broader implications of AI tools.
2. Advocate for inclusive and transparent policies.
3. Share accessible resources to foster public understanding.
