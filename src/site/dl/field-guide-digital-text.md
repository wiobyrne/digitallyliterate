Most arguments about whether students "still read" are arguments about what reading is. The honest answer is that the thing we used to call reading has split into three different practices, and pretending it hasn't is the source of most of our trouble.

I want to name them simply, because the names matter. *Slow reading* is what we do with a poem, an argument, a paragraph that costs something to understand. *Skimming* is what we do with most of what crosses our screens — purposeful, fast, looking for the one thing we need. *Browsing* is the thing in between, and it's the one that's hardest to teach because it looks like idleness from the outside.

A field guide is for the field. The fact that students do all three every hour, often without noticing, is not a problem to solve. It's the situation we teach inside.
